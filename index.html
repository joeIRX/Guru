Head-to-Head

Where Each System Falls Short

A fair look at every competitor — what they get right, and where IRX FOODSCORE® steps in to fill the gap.

Nutri-Score

C

A European front-of-pack label system using an A–E letter grade based on a fixed algorithm. Widely adopted in Europe but limited in scope and personalization.

Strengths
  • Simple A–E color label
  • EU-backed credibility
  • On-pack visibility
Limitations
  • No personalization
  • Misses vitamins/minerals
  • No portion context
  • Can favor processed foods
IRX FOODSCORE® Advantage

IRX delivers per-person scoring that accounts for what Nutri-Score ignores: your conditions, goals, medications, and the actual portion you're eating.

EWG Food Scores

C+

Environmental Working Group's database scores 80,000+ foods on nutrition, ingredient concerns, and processing — strong on chemical safety, weaker on personalization.

Strengths
  • Chemical & toxin focus
  • 80,000+ products
  • 3-factor scoring
Limitations
  • No user personalization
  • No health condition logic
  • No real-time updates
  • Environmental bias
IRX FOODSCORE® Advantage

IRX layers EWG-style safety data on top of a personalized health profile, giving you both ingredient safety AND whether it's right for your specific body.

Food Health Score

C

An academic-derived scoring model focused on overall diet quality. More comprehensive nutritionally than Nutri-Score but still population-level, not individual.

Strengths
  • Research-backed
  • Broader nutrient coverage
  • Diet quality focus
Limitations
  • Population-average scoring
  • No individual context
  • Limited app ecosystem
  • Academic, not consumer-facing
IRX FOODSCORE® Advantage

IRX applies the same evidence-based nutritional rigor but makes it actionable for consumers with a live, personalized score instead of an academic average.

Fooducate

C+

A grading app (A+ to D) that uses barcode scanning and a processing-aware algorithm. Popular for basic food tracking but limited in clinical depth.

Strengths
  • Processing-level awareness
  • Letter grade simplicity
  • Barcode scanning
Limitations
  • No personalization
  • Public data only
  • A-D scale lacks granularity
  • No health condition logic
IRX FOODSCORE® Advantage

IRX combines Fooducate's processing awareness with individual health profiles, medication flags, and a precise 100-point score that evolves with you.

Yuka

B

A French scan-and-score app combining Nutri-Score nutrition (60%), additive risk (30%), and organic certification (10%). Clean UI, no ads — but still one-size-fits-all.

Strengths
  • Additive risk scoring
  • Clean, no-ads experience
  • Simple excellent/good/poor
Limitations
  • Inherits Nutri-Score flaws
  • Zero personalization
  • No health condition logic
  • Europe-focused database
IRX FOODSCORE® Advantage

IRX respects Yuka's no-compromise integrity and builds on it — adding the personal health layer that transforms a universal score into your score.

MyFitnessPal

D+

The world's largest food tracking app with 14M+ items. Powerful for calorie logging, but research shows it consistently underestimates intake and offers no food quality scoring.

Strengths
  • Massive food database
  • Calorie & macro tracking
  • Wide device integration
Limitations
  • No food quality scoring
  • Underestimates intake ~18%
  • No ingredient safety data
  • Scanner behind paywall
IRX FOODSCORE® Advantage

IRX goes where MyFitnessPal stops — beyond calorie counting into food quality, ingredient safety, and health-specific scoring that actually guides better choices.
